About me

I'm a Ph.D. student (2025.9 - Present) in Zhejiang University.

My primary research interest lies in quantum thermodynamics, which seeks to define thermodynamic quantities—such as temperature, work, and heat—at the microscopic quantum scale, to elucidate their connections with classical, macroscopic thermodynamics, and to design devices that harness quantum advantage. Building on this foundation, I am also passionate about open quantum systems, quantum information, and quantum dynamics. Lately, my work has focused on experimental investigations of quantum thermodynamic processes using diamond nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers.
